## Executive Summary

Both NETL draft documents—Procedure 251.1-01 and Order 251.1D—demonstrate substantial alignment with DOE O 251.1E requirements for a departmental directives program. However, there are several high- and medium-severity compliance gaps and ambiguities, particularly regarding the definition and assignment of roles and responsibilities (especially for directive owners/OPRs), the treatment of exemptions/equivalencies, and the clarity of process steps. Some requirements from DOE O 251.1E are only partially addressed or are missing, and there are inconsistencies between the procedure and the order. Addressing these issues is necessary to ensure full compliance and operational clarity.

## Requirement-by-Requirement Findings

### 1. Roles and Responsibilities (DOE O 251.1E, Section 5)
- **DOE Requirement:** Explicitly defines and assigns roles for Secretary, Heads of Departmental Elements, Director Office of Management, DRB Members, Departmental Directives Program Manager, OPRs, DPCs, etc.
- **Order (251.1D):**
  - **Partially Met:** Defines Director, COO, Directives Manager, Office of Chief Counsel, QRB, and responsible organization. However, the role of OPR (Office of Primary Responsibility) is not clearly defined or mapped to NETL roles.
  - **Ambiguity:** The "responsible organization" is referenced but not clearly mapped to DOE OPR or its responsibilities.
- **Procedure (251.1-01):**
  - **Partially Met:** Lists responsibilities for Director, DRB, QRB, DM, COO, AD, SME, etc. However, the linkage to DOE OPR responsibilities is not explicit, and some roles (e.g., QRB) are not clearly defined in function.
  - **Missing:** No clear assignment of OPR duties as required by DOE O 251.1E Section 5.f.

### 2. Exemptions and Equivalencies (DOE O 251.1E, Section 3.d, 4.e, Appendix A)
- **DOE Requirement:** Must have a documented process for evaluating, granting, and documenting exemptions/equivalencies, with advice from OPR and legal review, and must not use exemptions/equivalencies in place of revising a directive.
- **Order:** 
  - **Not Addressed:** No explicit process or responsibilities for handling exemptions/equivalencies.
- **Procedure:** 
  - **Not Addressed:** No mention of exemptions/equivalencies process or responsibilities.

### 3. Directives Development Process (DOE O 251.1E, Section 4.b)
- **DOE Requirement:** Must use DRB-approved processes, timelines, and templates; process must be transparent, inclusive, and automated where possible.
- **Order:** 
  - **Partially Met:** References use of automation, DRB, and timelines, but lacks detail on DRB process and approval steps.
- **Procedure:** 
  - **Partially Met:** Outlines a multi-step process, but DRB and QRB roles and decision points are ambiguous. The process does not clearly state how DRB approval is obtained or what happens in case of impasse.

### 4. Unauthorized Directives (DOE O 251.1E, Section 4.g)
- **DOE Requirement:** Must define unauthorized directives and require reporting and resolution.
- **Order and Procedure:** 
  - **Met:** Both documents define unauthorized directives and require reporting and corrective action.

### 5. Prioritization and Review (DOE O 251.1E, Section 4.h, 5.b(13))
- **DOE Requirement:** Must provide for annual prioritization and periodic review of directives.
- **Order:** 
  - **Partially Met:** Refers to annual forecast and six-year review, but does not specify annual prioritization as required by DOE.
- **Procedure:** 
  - **Partially Met:** Describes annual forecast and six-year review, but does not clearly link to DOE’s annual prioritization requirement.

### 6. Training (DOE O 251.1E, Section 5.e(8))
- **DOE Requirement:** Must provide training on the directives program and processes.
- **Order:** 
  - **Partially Met:** States that no specific training is required, which conflicts with DOE O 251.1E.
- **Procedure:** 
  - **Met:** Requires DM to deliver process training to relevant personnel.

### 7. Record Control (DOE O 251.1E, Section 8)
- **DOE Requirement:** Must comply with DOE records schedules and maintain directive records.
- **Order and Procedure:** 
  - **Met:** Both documents reference DOE records schedules and provide for record retention.

### 8. Cross-Document Consistency
- **Finding:** There are inconsistencies in terminology, role definitions, and process steps between the order and the procedure (e.g., QRB/DRB functions, OPR/responsible organization).

## Gaps and Risks

- **High:** Lack of explicit mapping of DOE OPR responsibilities to NETL roles (Order & Procedure).
- **High:** No process or assignment of responsibilities for exemptions/equivalencies (Order & Procedure).
- **Medium:** Ambiguous or incomplete definition of QRB/DRB roles and approval steps (Order & Procedure).
- **Medium:** Training requirement in Order conflicts with DOE O 251.1E (Order).
- **Medium:** Inconsistent terminology and process steps between order and procedure (Cross-document).
- **Low:** Annual prioritization requirement is not clearly addressed (Order & Procedure).

## Suggested Revisions

### 1. Roles and Responsibilities
- **Order & Procedure (High):** Add a section that explicitly maps DOE OPR responsibilities (as per DOE O 251.1E Section 5.f) to the NETL "responsible organization" or another designated role. For example:
  > "The Responsible Organization shall serve as the Office of Primary Responsibility (OPR) for NETL directives, with duties including: proposing directives actions; following the process for developing or revising directives; developing CRDs as needed; adhering to prescribed formats; responding to comments; ensuring technical accuracy and relevance; and providing advice on exemptions and equivalencies."
- **Procedure (High):** Clarify and expand the responsibilities of the DM, SME, and AD to include all OPR functions per DOE O 251.1E.

### 2. Exemptions and Equivalencies
- **Order & Procedure (High):** Add a section describing the process for requesting, evaluating, and approving exemptions and equivalencies, including required documentation, OPR/legal review, and approval authority, consistent with DOE O 251.1E Appendix A.
  > "Requests for exemptions or equivalencies to directives must be submitted in writing, include justification, and be reviewed by the Responsible Organization (OPR) and Office of Chief Counsel before approval by the Director or designee. Exemptions/equivalencies must not be used in place of revising a directive."

### 3. Directives Development Process
- **Order & Procedure (Medium):** Clarify the roles and approval authorities of the DRB and QRB. Specify the process for resolving impasses and obtaining final approval, in line with DOE O 251.1E Section 4.b and 5.
  > "The DRB shall review and concur on all proposed directives actions. In the event of unresolved comments or impasse, the Director adjudicates or delegates resolution as appropriate."

### 4. Training
- **Order (Medium):** Revise to require training for all personnel involved in the directives process, consistent with DOE O 251.1E Section 5.e(8).
  > "The Directives Manager shall provide training on the directives program and its processes to all relevant personnel."

### 5. Annual Prioritization
- **Order & Procedure (Low):** Add language requiring annual prioritization of directives actions, as per DOE O 251.1E Section 4.h.
  > "The Responsible Organization shall provide the Directives Manager with planned directives actions for the coming year, to support annual prioritization."

### 6. Cross-Document Consistency
- **Order & Procedure (Medium):** Harmonize terminology and role definitions (e.g., OPR, Responsible Organization, QRB, DRB) and ensure process steps are described consistently in both documents.

## Priority Next Steps

1. **High:** Revise both the order and the procedure to explicitly define and assign OPR responsibilities and ensure all OPR duties from DOE O 251.1E are covered.
2. **High:** Add a documented process for exemptions and equivalencies, with clear responsibilities and approval steps.
3. **Medium:** Clarify DRB/QRB roles and approval processes, and ensure both documents describe these consistently.
4. **Medium:** Update the order to require training for directives program participants.
5. **Low:** Add language on annual prioritization of directives.
6. **Medium:** Review both documents for consistent terminology and process alignment.
7. **Re-review:** After revisions, conduct a follow-up compliance review to confirm all DOE O 251.1E requirements are fully addressed.
